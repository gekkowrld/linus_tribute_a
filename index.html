Linus Benedict Torvalds

Early Life and Background

Linus Torvalds was born on December 28, 1969, in Helsinki, Finland. He was born to Anna and Nils Torvalds. He was named after the Nobel Prize-winning American chemist Linus Pauling.

He started to write programs at a young age. Furthermore, he owned a Commodore VIC-20 in which he programmed in BASIC and Machine code

Passion for Programming

Linus began his journey into the world of programming at the young age. His early exposure to computers ignited a lifelong passion for coding and software development.

Creation of Linux

Linus Torvalds is most famous for his creation of the Linux kernel, an open-source operating system that has had a profound impact on the world of computing. Linux is a kernel that is used in various distributions like Ubuntu, Fedora and Arch Linux.

Creation of Git

In 2005, Git was developed by the Linux Community after the relationship with Bitkeeper broke down. [Bitkeeper is open source now]

Linus was the initial developer but it is now maintained by Junio C Hamano
